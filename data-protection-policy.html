engineersTech ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to the highest standards of data protection. This Data Protection Policy outlines our practices for collecting, processing, storing, and protecting personal and business data in compliance with the Bangladesh Digital Security Act 2018, the Bangladesh Cyber Security Act 2023, the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and other applicable international data protection frameworks.

1. Scope & Applicability

This policy applies to:

  • All personal data collected through our website, applications, and service delivery platforms
  • All client data processed during software development, deployment, and maintenance engagements
  • Data handled by employees, contractors, and third-party service providers acting on our behalf
  • Data processed for clients in Bangladesh, North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia-Pacific regions

2. Legal Basis for Processing

We process data under the following legal bases:

  • Contractual Necessity: Processing required to fulfill our service agreements and deliver contracted software solutions
  • Legitimate Interest: Processing for business operations, security monitoring, fraud prevention, and service improvement
  • Consent: Where explicitly provided by the data subject for marketing communications and non-essential cookies
  • Legal Obligation: Processing required to comply with Bangladesh tax regulations, trade licensing, and court orders

3. Categories of Data Processed

3.1 Client Business Data

During software development engagements, we may process:

  • Business contact information (names, emails, phone numbers, job titles)
  • Company registration and financial data necessary for ERP/accounting system development
  • User credentials and access logs for systems under development
  • Operational data provided for testing, migration, and integration purposes

3.2 Website Visitor Data

When you visit our website, we collect:

  • IP address, browser type, operating system, and device identifiers
  • Pages visited, referral URLs, and session duration
  • Form submissions (contact inquiries, RFP submissions, career applications)
  • Cookie identifiers and analytics data (via Google Analytics)

3.3 Employee & Contractor Data

We process personal data of our team members for employment administration, payroll, and project assignment purposes, in compliance with Bangladesh labor laws.

4. Bangladesh Cyber Security Act Compliance

As a Bangladesh-registered IT company (Trade License: TRAD/DNCC/025495/2025), we are fully subject to the Bangladesh Cyber Security Act 2023. Our compliance measures include:

  • Critical Information Infrastructure Protection: We maintain security protocols aligned with the Act's requirements for protecting digital infrastructure, including encrypted data storage, access controls, and incident response procedures
  • Unauthorized Access Prevention: All systems are protected against unauthorized access as defined under Sections 17-22 of the Act, with multi-factor authentication, role-based access control, and audit logging
  • Content & Data Integrity: We implement measures to prevent data tampering, identity fraud, and unauthorized modification of digital records as outlined in the Act
  • Reporting Obligations: We maintain procedures to report cybersecurity incidents to the Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory Commission (BTRC) and the Digital Security Agency as required by law
  • Record Retention: We retain processing records and security logs in compliance with the Act's requirements for audit and investigation purposes

5. GDPR Compliance (International Clients)

For clients and data subjects located in the European Economic Area (EEA), we comply with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Our GDPR-specific provisions include:

5.1 Data Subject Rights

Under GDPR, EEA data subjects have the following rights:

RightDescription
Access (Art. 15)Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you
Rectification (Art. 16)Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
Erasure (Art. 17)Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
Restriction (Art. 18)Request limitation of how we process your data
Portability (Art. 20)Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
Objection (Art. 21)Object to processing based on legitimate interest or direct marketing
Automated Decisions (Art. 22)Not be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer at info@engineerstechbd.com. We will respond within 30 days.

5.2 Lawful Basis for International Processing

When processing data of EEA residents, we rely on:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): We use EU-approved SCCs in our Data Processing Agreements with international clients
  • Adequacy Measures: We implement supplementary technical and organizational measures as recommended by the European Data Protection Board (EDPB)
  • Explicit Consent: For marketing communications and optional data collection, we obtain explicit, informed consent

6. Data Processing Agreements

When we process personal data on behalf of our clients (as a data processor), we enter into a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) that includes:

  • Clear definition of the scope, purpose, and duration of processing
  • Obligations to process data only on documented instructions from the data controller
  • Confidentiality commitments for all personnel with access to personal data
  • Technical and organizational security measures (see Section 7)
  • Procedures for assisting clients with data subject rights requests
  • Obligations to notify clients of data breaches within 72 hours
  • Requirements for sub-processor engagement and oversight
  • Procedures for data return or deletion upon contract termination

Our standard DPA is available upon request. Contact us at info@engineerstechbd.com.

7. Technical & Organizational Measures

We implement the following measures to protect data:

7.1 Technical Measures

  • Encryption: TLS 1.3 for data in transit; AES-256 for data at rest
  • Access Control: Role-based access control (RBAC), multi-factor authentication (MFA), and principle of least privilege
  • Network Security: Web Application Firewall (WAF), DDoS protection, intrusion detection/prevention systems (IDS/IPS)
  • Secure Development: OWASP-aligned secure coding practices, automated security scanning in CI/CD pipelines, regular penetration testing
  • Backup & Recovery: Daily automated encrypted backups with geo-redundant storage; tested disaster recovery procedures with defined RTO/RPO targets
  • Monitoring: 24/7 security monitoring and log aggregation with automated alerting for anomalous activities

7.2 Organizational Measures

  • Employee Training: Mandatory data protection and security awareness training for all staff, with annual refresher courses
  • Background Checks: Verification of personnel with access to sensitive client data
  • Incident Response Team: Dedicated team with documented procedures for security incident handling
  • Vendor Assessment: Security and privacy assessment of all third-party service providers before engagement
  • Clean Desk Policy: Physical and digital workspace security protocols
  • Separation of Duties: Critical operations require multi-person authorization

8. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, we will:

  1. Detect & Contain: Identify the breach scope and take immediate steps to contain the incident
  2. Assess Impact: Evaluate the risk to affected data subjects, including the nature and sensitivity of data involved
  3. Notify Authorities: Report to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach (GDPR Art. 33) and to the Bangladesh Digital Security Agency as required by the Cyber Security Act
  4. Notify Affected Parties: Inform affected data subjects without undue delay when the breach poses a high risk to their rights and freedoms (GDPR Art. 34)
  5. Remediate & Document: Implement corrective measures and maintain a detailed breach register for audit purposes

10. Cross-Border Data Transfers

As a Bangladesh-based company serving international clients, data may be transferred across borders. We ensure lawful transfers through:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses: EU-approved SCCs incorporated into client agreements for EEA data transfers
  • Adequate Safeguards: Technical measures including encryption and pseudonymization applied to all cross-border data flows
  • Data Localization: Where required by client policy or local regulation, we deploy infrastructure in the client's preferred region (AWS, Azure, or GCP availability zones)
  • Transfer Impact Assessments: Conducted for transfers to jurisdictions without adequacy decisions, per EDPB Recommendations 01/2020
